This guide will walk you through creating your first customized WooCommerce My Account menu. By the end, you'll know how to add custom items, reorder the menu, and add icons.

## Access the menu builder

<Steps>
  <Step title="Navigate to settings">
    Go to **WooCommerce → Settings** in your WordPress admin, then click the **My Account Menu** tab.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Review the interface">
    You'll see the drag & drop menu builder with a live preview sidebar showing your current menu items. The default WooCommerce items (Dashboard, Orders, Downloads, etc.) are already loaded.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Info>
The live preview shows exactly how your menu will appear to customers on the My Account page.
</Info>

## Create your first custom menu item

Let's create a custom "Help Center" page in the My Account menu.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Click 'Add Item'">
    At the top of the menu builder, click the **Add Item** button to open the item editor modal.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Enter basic details">
    Fill in the following fields:
    - **Title**: `Help Center`
    - **Type**: Select `Endpoint` (this creates a new My Account page)
    - **Endpoint Slug**: The slug auto-generates as `help-center` based on your title
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Add content">
    In the **Endpoint Content** field, add your page content. You can use:
    - Plain HTML: `<h2>Need Help?</h2><p>Contact our support team at support@example.com</p>`
    - WordPress shortcodes: `[contact-form-7 id="123"]`
    - Mixed content: Combine HTML and shortcodes
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Save the item">
    Click **Save** to add the item to your menu. You'll see it appear in the menu builder list.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
Endpoint slugs are automatically generated from the title. You can customize the slug if needed, but avoid using slugs that conflict with existing WooCommerce endpoints.
</Tip>

## Reorder menu items

Let's move your new Help Center item to a more prominent position.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Grab the item">
    Click and hold the **drag handle** (six dots icon) on the left side of any menu item.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Drag to position">
    Drag the item up or down to your desired position. For example, drag "Help Center" to appear right after "Dashboard".
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Drop the item">
    Release to drop the item in place. The live preview updates instantly.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Save changes">
    Scroll down and click **Save Changes** to persist your menu order.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Add an icon to your menu item

Icons make your menu more visually appealing and easier to scan.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Edit the item">
    Click the **Edit** (pencil icon) button on your "Help Center" item to reopen the editor modal.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Open icon picker">
    Scroll to the **Icon** section and click the **icon picker** button to see available icons.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Select an icon">
    Choose an icon from the visual picker (36+ popular icons), or enter a custom Dashicons or Font Awesome class:
    - **Dashicons**: `dashicons-sos` (for help/support)
    - **Font Awesome**: `fa-solid fa-circle-question`
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Save and preview">
    Click **Save**. The icon appears next to your menu item in the live preview.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
As of version 1.3.0, the plugin uses WordPress Dashicons exclusively for zero additional weight. Font Awesome references in the codebase are from earlier versions.
</Note>

## Create an external link

You can also add external links to resources outside the My Account area.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Add a new item">
    Click **Add Item** again.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Configure as link">
    - **Title**: `Product Documentation`
    - **Type**: Select `Link`
    - **URL**: `https://docs.example.com`
    - **Open in new tab**: Check this option to open the link in a new browser tab
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Add icon (optional)">
    Select an icon like `dashicons-book` or `dashicons-media-document`.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Save">
    Click **Save** to add the external link to your menu.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Test your menu on the frontend

<Steps>
  <Step title="Save all changes">
    Make sure you've clicked **Save Changes** at the bottom of the settings page.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Visit My Account page">
    Navigate to your WooCommerce My Account page (typically at `/my-account/`).
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Check custom endpoint">
    Click your "Help Center" menu item. You should see your custom content at `/my-account/help-center/`.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
If you see a 404 error on your custom endpoint, go to **Settings → Permalinks** and click **Save Changes** to flush rewrite rules. This is rarely needed but may be required on some hosting configurations.
</Warning>

## Common customizations

### Add a badge to an item

Badges help highlight new features or important sections:

1. Edit any menu item
2. Scroll to the **Badge** section
3. Choose **Static badge** and enter text like `New`, `Pro`, or `Hot`
4. Or choose **Dynamic counter** to show order count or download count
5. Save the item

### Add a separator

Separators create visual groups in your menu:

1. Click **Add Item**
2. Set **Type** to `Separator`
3. Optionally add a **Title** (appears as a group label)
4. Drag to position the separator between groups
5. Save

### Hide items from specific roles

Role-based visibility lets you show items only to certain user types:

1. Edit any menu item
2. Scroll to **Visible to Roles**
3. Check the roles that should see this item (e.g., only "Administrator" and "Shop Manager")
4. Leave all unchecked to make the item visible to everyone
5. Save

<Tip>
This works with custom roles from membership plugins, subscription plugins, and any other plugin that registers WordPress user roles.
</Tip>
